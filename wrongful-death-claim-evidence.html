WRONGFUL DEATH CLAIM: WHAT RECORDS ARE REQUIRED FOR EVIDENCE

  1. Home
  2. Wrongful Death Claim: What Records Are Required for Evidence

The loss of a loved one is an emotionally challenging experience, and when that loss is the result of someone else's negligence or misconduct, pursuing a wrongful death claim becomes a necessary step toward justice. In such cases, gathering the right evidence is crucial to building a strong case. This blog explores the essential records needed for evidentiary support in wrongful death claims.

Wrongful Death Lawsuit

A wrongful death lawsuit is a legal action that is filed by the survivors or beneficiaries of a person who has died due to the negligence or intentional actions of another party. The purpose of a wrongful death case is to seek compensation for the survivors' damages and losses as an outcome of the death.

Wrongful death claims may result from a variety of situations, including:

  • Negligence: If someone's negligent actions or failure to take reasonable precautions lead to the death of another person, a wrongful death claim may be filed.
  • Intentional Harm: If someone intentionally causes the death of another person, whether through assault, battery, or another intentional act, a wrongful death lawsuit may be pursued.
  • Product Liability: If a defective product causes a person's death, the manufacturer or distributor of the product may be held responsible in a wrongful death lawsuit.
  • Medical Malpractice: If a healthcare professional's negligence or medical malpractice results in a patient's death, the surviving family may file a wrongful death claim.
  • Workplace Accidents: If a person dies because of a workplace accident, the family may file a wrongful death lawsuit against the employer or other responsible parties.

What Records are Needed for Evidence in a Wrongful Death Claim?

Death Certificate

The cornerstone of any wrongful death claim is the official death certificate. This document provides essential details such as the cause and manner of death, and it establishes the legal basis for the claim. Ensuring the accuracy and completeness of the death certificate is fundamental to the success of the case.

Medical Records

Detailed medical records are critical in establishing a link between the negligent actions and the cause of death. This includes hospital records, treatment plans, medication history, and any other medical documentation that sheds light on the deceased's health condition leading up to the fatal incident.

Accident Reports

If the death resulted from an accident, obtaining official accident reports is vital. This could involve reports from law enforcement agencies, workplace incident reports, or any other documentation that outlines the circumstances surrounding the fatal event.

Eyewitness Statements

Eyewitness accounts can provide invaluable perspectives on the events leading to the wrongful death. Statements from individuals who witnessed the incident or were present at the scene can contribute to a more comprehensive understanding of what transpired.

Expert Opinions

In many wrongful death claims, seeking expert opinions is crucial. Expert witnesses, such as medical professionals or accident reconstruction specialists, can offer their insights and opinions on the causation of the death. In court, their testimony may be very important.

Employment Records

If the wrongful death occurred in the workplace, employment records become key evidence. These may include work schedules, safety protocols, training records, and any evidence demonstrating the employer's responsibility for maintaining a safe working environment.

Financial Documents

Wrongful death claims often include a component for financial losses suffered by the survivors. Financial documents such as tax returns, pay stubs, and records of the deceased's financial contributions can substantiate the economic impact of the loss.

Insurance Policies

Documentation related to the deceased's insurance policies, including life insurance, health insurance, or any policies relevant to the incident, can be essential for calculating damages and establishing liability.

Communication Records

Correspondence such as emails, text messages, or letters can provide context to the relationship between the deceased and the liable party. This may include evidence of negligence, prior incidents, or any communication that contributes to establishing liability.

Autopsy Reports

In cases where an autopsy was conducted, the autopsy report can be a critical piece of evidence. It provides detailed information on the examination of the body, contributing to a thorough understanding of the cause of death.

Wrongful Death Attorney

A wrongful death attorney is a lawyer who specializes in cases where a person's death has occurred due to the negligence, recklessness, or intentional misconduct of another party. These cases typically involve situations such as car accidents, medical malpractice, workplace accidents, defective products, or criminal activities.

Wrongful death laws vary by jurisdiction, but in general, they allow certain family members or beneficiaries to bring a legal action against the responsible party for damages. Damages may include compensation for medical expenses, funeral costs, lost income, loss of companionship, and other related expenses.

Wrongful death attorneys help the surviving family members navigate the legal process, investigate the circumstances surrounding the death, gather evidence, negotiate with insurance companies, and represent their clients in court if a lawsuit is filed. These attorneys play a crucial role in seeking justice and financial compensation for the family members who have lost a loved one due to someone else's wrongful actions.

Conclusion

Building a strong wrongful death claim requires meticulous attention to detail and a comprehensive collection of relevant records. By gathering these essential pieces of evidence, you not only strengthen your case but also contribute to the pursuit of justice for your loved one. Consulting with legal professionals experienced in wrongful death claims can help guide you through the process and ensure that no critical evidence is overlooked.
